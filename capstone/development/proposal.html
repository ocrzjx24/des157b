jason's capstone project proposal

--

Most recently, I've been fascinated by old-school futures. Primarily I'm thinking about sci-fi media and how different it is in each era. From the cautionary tales of 60s sci-fi to the extremely optimistic Frutiger Aero aesthetics of the early 2000s, there has always been a lot of speculation about the future (or now, present) that has been both very wrong and very right, and I think this is a really interesting concept to explore. I'm not fully sure what that might entail, but I do want to lean into an interactive narrative concept. Perhaps users can explore the different forms of sci-fi, discovering both their pros and cons and how they have differed from reality? In pertinence to the class, I suppose this idea draws most from the lenses of speculative design and utopian / dystopian narratives.

I think that I need to explore more libraries to determine what might elevate my idea, but the ones that I used in the Library Liberty mini-studio (NEAT Gradients and Framer Motion) were very strong, easy-to-use, general libraries that I believe could have a place in my final project. Animate on Scroll also seems promising to me. Overall, the goal is to build an interactive experience that plays into some sort of story-telling. I think currently it is still in many ways too ambiguous for me to say for certain what I want to incorporate and how. The concept in my mind is super broad, and I don't know yet what more specific things I want to explore as I go.

Definitely I think I will simply need to ideate more or find inspiration through talking to friends about the idea. My target audience could remain broad but I think I want to focus on my generation (part of me just wants to create something my friends will think is cool, but I think more importantly, I want to provoke thought). I think that a lot of Gen Z kids haven't read older sci-fi books (and the only reason I have is because of a little phase I had in high school) and that in general, Gen Z is very passive towards even heavy negative change in the world. I want my project to somehow subvert expectations and allow people to not only learn a bit about what past generations expected the world to look like, but also bring light to some of the pretty alarming changes occurring in the world (both under-the-radar and blatantly in front of us). I also want the project to be a reflection of how I currently think and I hope that future me can look back and be proud of the vision.
