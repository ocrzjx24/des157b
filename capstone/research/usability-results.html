results after usability testing

After performing some user testing in today's class, I realized and understood a few things about my project, and definitely gained more insight towards what I should be doing.

1. Several users were initially confused by either the premise or the function of my site, and this was typically attributed to a lack of context when the site loads. There is little instruction and while the general design and flow were described as intuitive, there could be more text or panels that explain the purpose of the site and what users ought to do.

2. While my site is definitely unfinished, it seems that the purpose of each book's side panel wasn't super well understood. Some users thought that it would have a form inside of it, while others didn't immediately know why predictions were even brought up. While context will help, I was definitely forced to think about what I want the panels to display. I think I want to include a cover / graphic of the book alongside graphs and charts for stats, then perhaps a question to add an interactive element instead of making it just static.

3. Finally, I received some feedback about general readability. Users liked the color palette and didn't have too much trouble with navigating the site, but there were some comments on text being small, hard-to-read against the dark background, and at times generally inaccessible. I'll definitely change the colors and test them to address these concerns.
